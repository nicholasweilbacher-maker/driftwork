## Course Overview

The course runs across two semesters, covering foundational spoken Irish (Gaeilge). All classes follow a consistent structure: a **Seanfhocal** (proverb), textbook work, paired conversation practice, and supplemental handouts.

### Curriculum Progression at a Glance

| Phase | Topics |
|---|---|
| **Early (Nov 2025)** | Weather, Seasons, Nouns & Gender, the Copula, Professions, Personality |
| **Mid (Dec 2025)** | Verbs — 3 groups + 11 Irregular Verbs, Present Tense (Aimsir Láithreach) |
| **Semester 2 (Jan–Feb 2026)** | Present Tense practice, Days/Numbers/Countries, Eclipsis (Urú), Prepositional Pronouns |
| **Late (Feb–Mar 2026)** | Possessive Pronouns, revision, Past Tense (Aimsir Chaite) |
| **Final (Mar–Apr 2026)** | Past Tense application, reading stories, personal profiles |

### Class 05 — 8 December 2025 *(makeup class)*
**Seanfhocal:** *Is fearr an tsláinte ná an táinte* — "Health is better than wealth" (Táinte = old Irish for cattle/herd = wealth)

**Topics Covered:**
- **Na Briathra** (verbs) — 3 categories:
  - **An Chéad Réimniú** (First Conjugation — short root verbs)
  - **An Dara Réimniú** (Second Conjugation — longer verbs, often ending in -igh/-aigh)
  - **Na Briathra Neamhrialta** (Irregular Verbs — 11 verbs)
- The **leathan le leathan, caol le caol** rule (broad with broad, slender with slender)
- **11 Irregular Verbs:**
  - *Abair* (to say), *Beir* (to catch/carry), *Bí* (to be), *Clois* (to hear), *Déan* (to do/make), *Faigh* (to get), *Feic* (to see), *Ith* (to eat), *Tabhair* (to give), *Tar* (to come), *Téigh* (to go)
- Started **An Aimsir Láithreach** (present tense) — covered up to slide 6; to be finished after Christmas
- **Christmas vocab** (An Nollaig)

**Class break:** Christmas. Resumes **12 January 2026**.
